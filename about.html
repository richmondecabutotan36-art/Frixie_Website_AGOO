About Agoo

Discover the history, mission, and vision of the oldest town in La Union

History of Agoo

Established in 1578, Agoo is considered the oldest town in La Union and was formerly part of Pangasinan. The town’s name is believed to have come from the pine-like “aroo” trees that once lined its riverbanks. Agoo played an important role in early evangelization in Northern Luzon through Franciscan missionaries who established a church dedicated to St. Francis of Assisi.

Other religious orders such as the Augustinians helped develop education, religious traditions, and infrastructure. Agoo also became home to the Basilica Minore of Our Lady of Charity, declared in 1981. During World War II, the town experienced major historical events, especially in Barangay Macalva.

Mission

It is our mission to deliver and sustain socio-economic, health, environment, and infrastructure support services and programs to improve the quality of life of all Agoeños.

Vision

By 2030, Agoo shall be the center of commerce, education, and tourism in La Union with God-fearing and empowered citizens, progressive peaceful communities, and a sustainable environment achieved through transparent and participatory governance.
